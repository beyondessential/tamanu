# 2.2 Proposed solution: Overview

BES proposes to establish Zimbabwe's National Shared Health Record on Tamanu, a mature, free and open-source clinical platform already in use as the national health record in six countries. The SHR holds a longitudinal clinical record for each citizen, covering diagnoses, problems, medications, allergies, immunisations, laboratory results, vital signs and encounter summaries drawn from every facility they attend. Tamanu's Patient Master Index (PMI) ties each record to the right person across the health system.

Architecturally, Tamanu sits at the centre of the Digital Health Exchange as the OpenHIE-aligned Client Registry and clinical repository. Zimbabwe's core systems (iMpilo, eLMIS, LIMS and DHIS2, and in time the private-sector EMRs) connect once to this central hub and exchange both patient identity and clinical data through a standard HL7 FHIR R4 API, rather than each system integrating directly with every other. This hub-and-spoke model is a direct answer to the scaling problem the Ministry identifies in the ToR: it replaces the unsustainable web of point-to-point links with a single connection per system to shared national registries, through which any authorised system can reach the data it needs. The FHIR layer runs in Tamanu production today, powering live integrations across laboratory, pharmacy, imaging, billing and reporting systems in multiple countries.

BES proposes a centrally-hosted SHR as the authoritative national record, hosted on-premises in MoHCC infrastructure. Tamanu is built as a distributed hub-and-spoke system that scales to a national population and 1,000+ facilities by balancing processing and data across the network and, where connectivity is unreliable, running close to the point of care: a facility can operate its own Tamanu node entirely offline and synchronise automatically with the national record once a connection is available, with the central server itself scaling horizontally through parallel, load-balanced processes. This gives the Ministry one coherent architecture that serves both well-connected referral hospitals and remote clinics, without the fragility of a bolt-on offline mode, a capability no purely cloud-hosted or standalone FHIR-server solution can match.

Tamanu is a complete, proven clinical platform, so the SHR puts data to work at the point of care: allergies and current medications surfaced whenever a record is opened, a unified view of each patient's history regardless of where care was delivered, and FHIR-based patient summaries for referrals and continuity of care, all governed by granular role-based access. It is free and open-source, with no licensing fees or vendor lock-in, and BES hands over full source code, documentation and training so that the Ministry owns and can extend the system indefinitely.
