# 2.2 Proposed solution — Overview

BES proposes to establish Zimbabwe's National Shared Health Record on Tamanu, deployed in its Patient Master Index (PMI) configuration. Rather than building a new platform from the ground up, the Ministry adopts a mature, open-source system already in use as the national health record in six countries and configures it to the role the ToR sets out: a single, authoritative, longitudinal record of each citizen's care, and the trusted source of patient identity for every system connected to the Digital Health Exchange.

Architecturally, Tamanu sits at the centre of the exchange as the OpenHIE-aligned Client Registry and PMI. Zimbabwe's core systems — iMpilo, eLMIS, LIMS and DHIS2, and in time the private-sector EMRs — connect once to this central hub and exchange data through a standard HL7 FHIR R4 API, rather than each system integrating directly with every other. This hub-and-spoke model is a direct answer to the scaling problem the Ministry identifies in the ToR: it replaces the unsustainable web of point-to-point links with a single connection per system to shared national registries, through which any authorised system can reach the data it needs. The FHIR layer is not a specification we intend to build for this project — it runs in Tamanu production today, with more than ten live FHIR integrations across laboratory, pharmacy, imaging and reporting systems.

BES proposes a centrally-hosted SHR as the authoritative national record, consistent with the ToR's centralised client-server architecture and on-premises MoHCC hosting. Tamanu's distinguishing strength, however, is that the same platform is offline-capable by design: where connectivity is unreliable, a facility can run its own Tamanu server that operates entirely offline and synchronises automatically with the national record once a connection is available. This gives the Ministry one coherent architecture that serves both well-connected referral hospitals and remote clinics, without the fragility of a bolt-on offline mode — a capability no purely cloud-hosted or standalone FHIR-server solution can match.

Because Tamanu is a complete, proven clinical platform rather than a bare repository, the SHR arrives with the clinical depth the ToR requires: a unified longitudinal patient view, robust patient matching and deduplication underpinning the unique patient identifier, allergies and medications surfaced at the point of care, immunisation and maternal-and-child-health records, and FHIR-based patient summaries for referrals and continuity of care. It is free and open-source, with no licensing fees or vendor lock-in, and BES hands over full source code, documentation and training so that the Ministry owns and can extend the system indefinitely.
